Mercury

UX, UI, Research

A phone application that increases the uses experience of in-store retail shopping by accommodating online functionality to be used in-store.

In-store retail shopping has become less popular due to the convenience of the online equivalent. This application should bridge the gap between online and in-person by enhancing the user's experience of in-store shopping by allowing them access to online functionality. The app should allow users to enjoy in-store retail shopping with the app adding additional features to increase the experience. The main issues this app is looking to resolve are common issues in-store retail have such as long queues, lack of stock availability, etc...

Client
This was a passion project that I worked on outside of my current UX position

Role
I was the entire team for this project, covering all of the research, testing, Ux, UI, etc...

High Level Goals

  1. Create a system that would improve the in-store shopping experience for shoppers by giving them access to easy-to-use online features

  2. Creating a way that will speed up check out time, with the goal to decress queining time by over 50%

  3. Reduce the amount of wasted user’s time and frustration by introducing systems that allows users to find what they want as fast as possible

Main Challenges

  1. Creating a system that could be rolled out into all highstreet clothing store will minimal effort and expense from the shop

  2. Creating a system that will increase functionality but won’t increase the chance of shoplifting

  3. Creating a branding system that will stand out as a unique brand while working with all of the product shots from a range of different companies

Process

The process I used to complete projects include the following

User Research

After looking at what apps are currently out there and using some of their research as a starting point I was able to categorize the main users of the application into three key user personas.


  1. The day out shopper
    A user who enjoy and make a day of shopping, enjoys the experience

  2. The Parent
    A user who is going shopping for a purpose rather than an experience

  3. The Student
    This would take into account all of the people who sit in the middle of these two, have a purpose but are going to enjoy the time

Site map

After having an understanding of the users I then moved to mapping out the Application architecture, firstly the app map for the application and then the information structure.
I focused on:


  1. Creating a top-level navigation system that would not waste any of the users time, making sure not to have excess steps and get the users to where they want to go, quickly

  2. Creating a desgin system that would be recognisable to users to make the system feel intuitive

  3. Creating strong Information architeture that would require very little back stepping from the user, so all of the relevant information is all groupedcorrectly

  4. Allow the application to be a secondary focus for the user, so they are able to focus on their in-store experience

  2. A user checking if there is an item in stock

Wireframes


With all of this information behind be, I felt that I could accurately make wireframes that could solve the issues that i’ve outlined at the start of the project but with the user now in mind I could make more informed decisions about the design.

Steps


  1. Hand drawing wireframes with lots of variation in design to have all aspects of the design throught out

  2. Then move the best working wireframes into Figma, starting with low fidelity, increasing it every step, a imageless, grey wireframe that can be tested

  3. A loop of testing and reworking wireframes until the designs are to a point where the UI needs to be considered for any further changes.

User Testing Affinity Mapping

Layout (Grid)


The Grid uses a symmetrical 6 column grid that allows the designs to stay symmetrical but flexible for all of the small icons needed throughout the design.

Due to the whole app being only mobile the grid doesn't need to change. The layout grid padding will change to fit the phone it is being displayed on. The base grid will have 50px colomns with 8px gutters that are able to scale up and down for the size of the phone screen.

Colour Scheme


The colour scheme has been kept neutral to only using black and white, but to give the app a sense of branding I have also introduced an accent orange colour throughout the design.

The reason black and white have been kept as the main colours is to not take away from the photos, as these are the main focus point of the screen. The neutral palette has also been used so that this application can work with a range of different companies, so their branding and images won't have negative effects on the application.

Iconography


The Iconography used throughout the app is comparable with travel and shopping applications. The main goal to create consistency throughout the icons and making them easy to interpret

Typography


All items in the type scale use Futura PT, making use of a varity of weight available, Bold, Medium, Book and Light.

Futura PT is a pro variation of the 1927 Paul Renner font “Futura”. It has a stylish and modern feel while being very legible and scalable to the user's needs. The reason I used the “PT” version is due to the increase in font variations as well as the font having a more consistent x-height throughout

Motion

I have mocked up the motion design in key aspects of the application to show the user's experience. The main purpose of these actions were to provide feedback to the user after they have completed actions and to introduce usability features that will function in intuitive ways without having to clutter their UI
