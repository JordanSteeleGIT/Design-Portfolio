Impact

UX, UI, Web Design, Research

Impact is a web design project to deliver more a brand they conveys the companies values to the clients

Impact is an energy company that specialises in boiler, insulation, and heat solutions. They needed a site that communicated to the customers that they are trustworthy and a professional brand. They had some prior branding and wanted to keep it but create a clean and modern-looking website. The main challenge for this project was creating a site that reflected what the brand was about while keeping within their outdated branding. (The logo has been changed for legal purposes)

Client
Impact Energy Ltd.

Role
I was the entire team for this project, coving all of research, testing, UX, UI, etc..

High level goals

  1. Create a clean, modern and professional website that communicates the values of the company to the consumer

  2. The only had a lot of employee turn over, there is a need to create a system to display jobs and an application section that can be controlled on the WordPress backend

  3. To create a scalable system that could be used to create further pages on the site without the input of a designer

Main challenges

  1. Creating a clean site white adhering to their branding which is very outdated

  2. The company had assets they wanted to used that where outdated and normally pixeled and wanted them using so they had to be recreated and fit into the design

  3. To create a site that works as a funnel to get the users to go to other sites the company has in place to sell their products

Process

The process I used to complete projects include the following

Research


To begin I started to look at the competitors to see how they present their brand to clients. I was also conscious that their competitors try and sell from their site, rather this site will redirect users to the sites they use to sell their product. I focused on the customer journey they use and the language they use to communicate their message.

Focusing on:

  1. Who was going to be the main competor in the area that this site would have to try and take clients away from

  2. How they lead the users journey throughout the site

  3. Who they have designed for and how they have designed for them

User personas

I moved onto making user personas for the site. I focused on making personas that would cover the average people that are going to visit the site and their goals of the site.


  1. The Local homeower should cover the vast majority of users and their goals

  2. The Young couple are the second largest group who will trying to find a trust worthy contractor

  3. The employee will be the least dense category yet one that needs to looked at in detail due to clients request

Site maps


After having a firm sense of who I was designing for and what their goals are going to be when using the website I moved on to create a minimal site map that would direct all of the users to their goal desitnation with minal time lost.

Site flow


Finally I decided the last step that needed to be taken before moving to wireframes was to create a site flow for the user. I did this as I wanted to make sure that there is no areas of the site where the users would feel they are wasting time and would have to restart

Wireframes


With all of the information behind the wireframes, I was able to understand what the customer goals were going to be and I was able to focus on a few key aspects of the design. When making the wireframes it was important to focus on three key aspects

Focus


  1. Having an impactful landing section, to bring the users into the design and create a strong first impression

  2. Creating an image lead design that can communicate to the customer who they are

  3. Trying to communicate to the customer that they have been around for a while and having a loyal customer base.

Client feedback


When making this project I had regular contact with the clients, pitching what I had done and the direction I was going in. Making sure to keep them involved in the process and not left out. I wanted to make sure that they had everything they wanted to be included in the site and a site they would be happy within the end. This even resulted in creating some access I advised against so they could see they wouldn't work. Throughout the feedback, they had three main points that were the focus.

Main points

  1. They wanted to keep the brand colours throughout but liked the idea of having a mainly white and neutral colour palette

  2. Wanted to drive what they do and their reviews to the customer

  3. The clients liked the idea of the site being image-led, but wanted to include as much branding and brand establishing sections as possible

Layout (Grid)


Desktop

The Grid uses a symmetrical 12 column grid, this allows for the desktop site to a fluid feel. But due to some of the pages being split in half, it allows two 6 column grids so everything works and doesn't feel cramped.

Mobile

The mobile grid uses a 5 column grid. This allowed the site to be able to have space in the middle of the page to have large images which were needed for the design. Having a 5 column grid on mobile from a 12 on desktop, the design needed to make sure it doesn't look too asymmetrical as that would create a disconnect between the desktop and mobile.

Colour scheme


The colour scheme was already set by the clients as this was their previous branding. A baby blue was added to the site as this gave the site background some depth instead of keeping it all white as the client initially wanted.

Iconography


The Icons used throughout the design have been used to illustrate points to the consumer in a very simple way. It has been used in a way that saves space and the user can interpret the information easily.

Typography


All items in the type scale use Bernino Sans, making use of two weights, Bold and Light.

Bernino Sans, also seen as JAF Bernino Sans was designed by Tim Ahrens & Shoko Mugikura in 2012. The font has a clean modern look they reflect professionalism.

Due to the clean nature of the font, it is very legible which was important as we outlined at the start of the project an older generation will be using the site.

Nav bar


To create a navbar that could accurately communicate to customers what the product is, I decided to use icons within the navbar which also had an effective effect on them. This hopefully should make the options more attractive and easier for the users to understand what they are clicking on as well create sections to the website

Final iteration


The final version of the design uses a minimal image-led design, with limited colours to try and convey the message of the brand to the consumer. The site uses sliders to show a range of information as efficiently as possible. It also has added features to make it more of a coherent brand, such as changing the highlight on the site to be green, to represent the brand.

The imagery used in the site was used to either reflect the life style that the client is trying to project or images of their products.

Measuring success

Measuring the Success of this project can not be done yet as the site is not live, it is currently in development. Though I can to a degree measure the success in the feedback from the client. Other ways I will measure the success of the site will be:


  1. Amount of users going from this site to the other sites they use to sell their products

  2. The amount of the jobs listed get applied for

  3. The number of emails that the client receives from uses on the site

Reflection

When looking back on this project I am not totally happy with the final product, I feel like there are too many elements that float and are not anchored to anything, and feel the design can have a cramped feel to it in some sections. This being said these issues and outlined fixes to these issues have been brought up to the client who said they preferred the current version rather than a fixed one.

I have hope that when it comes out of dev, I will be able to change some elements to try and make it a more well-rounded site.
