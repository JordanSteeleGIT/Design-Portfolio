Inside Investor

UX, UI, Web Design, MVT, A/B Testing, Lead Generation

Inside Investor is a financial investment company that had low conversion rate on their site so we re-designed and prefromed A/B Testing on the site

Inside Investor is a financial investment company that had a very low conversion rate as well as a low user retention time on their site. They also felt their design was outdated and need to be refreshed. The goal of this task was to create a more professional and trustworthy brand as well as increase the conversion rate of users entering the site and submitting a form. The main challenge of this project was to create a system that allowed uses to enter an amount as easily as possible but didn't suggest any amount beforehand so they invest what they are comfortable with.

Client
Inside Investor

Role
Head of A/B Test and research
Head of mobile design
Part of the UX/UI Team

High level goals

  1. Re-Design Inside Investor to a modern, slick and professional site that matches the brand and builds trust with the user

  2. Increase the conversion rate of first-time users on the site to submitting a request form

  3. Increase user retention for first time users onto the site and increase the length of time users send on the site

Main challenges

  1. Creating a top CTA that will allow users to select investing amount as easy as possible but in a way that doesn't influence them in how much they should be investing

  2. Create a design that is aesthetically appealing to the user but suggests they should click and read through the information that is being presented

  3. Creating a top CTA that allowed a user to be able to make an enquiry as fast as possible (ClIent Request)

Conversion rate A/B testing

We kept the original slider CTA on the re-design as the client informed us that they had done a previous test and found that to be the best. The re-design increased the conversion rate by 12% from 7% to 19%. This was of users going onto the site for the first time and clicking through the first CTA.

Where this is an improvement we didn't have any data other than this one number of first time users clicking the CTA and sending a form so from previous experience it felt like this number was low for the design.

We decided to A/B test different CTA on the landing page to see if we could increase this rate. After research and previous experience on a similar site, we opted for 3 different CTA’s and decided to test a more impactful colour scheme to see if we would increase the conversion rate.

A/B tests

Amount enterer

Drop down amount

Graphically striking

We used the slider CTA but with more striking colours on the landing image too see if that would increase the conversion rate

NOTE

These test were run at different times so we had high enough volumes of traffic to make educated decisions on which of the CTA/landing pages worked the best

CTA findings

32%

Single button CTA

we found out that the single button CTA had the highest conversion rate at around 32%, peaking at 45% for a day. We also found the amount being entered was on the lower side but still within the client's range.

25%

Drop down amount CTA

We found that the drop-down CTA and amount enterer CTA kept the conversion rate around 25% but found that the amounts being enter were lower by average than the other CTA’s and when relaying this to the client they informed us that they didn't want to go in this directions.

23%

Slider CTA

The slider CTA over the time had an average of around 23% conversion rate but had the highest amounts entered when the user completed and submitted the form.

18%

Amount enterer CTA

Prefroming the worst, the amount enterer had an increase in conversion rate by 18% but due to the control group of keeping the orgial slifer which averaged at 23% this was the only one that decreased from the control group

30%

Graphicallly appealing

Finally, we found out that the graphically striking landing page did increase the sliders rate from 23% to 30% but we found that it had a much higher amount of users entering the maximum number with fake details, resulting in those leads being worthless.

Conclusion


To conclude we decided to go with the single buton CTA for the project due to the conversion rate and the amounts being entered within in the ranage fo the customer. We also used this as a case study for future projects of similar types so we could have high base conversion rates without having it run A/B testing on every site.

User retension time A/B test

The clients wanted to increase the amount of time that users spent on the site. The re -design of the site did this with the average time the user spent on the site being 3:30. We felt that for the landing page of the site this time was appropriate. The issue came that users were not clicking on any of the links on the page.
The client specifically wanted users to click through the different sectors they are in, this being the main pannel that the users would use to do this.

Hypothisis

Due to the success that the graphically striking landing section had on conversion rate we wanted to go with an image-based, visually impactful layout. We wanted to bring the user's eye straight to the options and make it standout from the rest of the design.

This original pitch was turned down by the client as they didnt want to stray too far from the orginal design. We decided that only a hoover effect was going to be the most approate way of keeping the design but increasing the amount of people clicking on the options

Due to only changing the hoover state we decided to run more A/B testing to see if we could increase the numbers as effectivly as possible

A/B test results

We were able to increase the number of people clicking through these options with a full darkened image with white text hoover affect on each of the options.

We were able to increase the number of users clicking through these options by 215% with the full image variation, The semi-circle hoover effect was close behind with 190% increase of people clicking through. This would have been interesting if we were also to run for an extended amount of time to see if the results changed

Measuring success

  1. The first measure of success will be if we did what we were contracted to, increase conversion rate. With the orginal rate being 7% and by the end of the project we had the rate at being 32%, I would consider this project a success

  2. The second would be to increase people clicking through the different investment industries. We increased this by 215% from the re-design which we suspect was high than the original, they had no data on this so we can't say for sure. I was also consider this project in that aspect a success too

  3. Finally, the last measure would be if we increased the amount of time users spent on the site. We averaged 3:30 - 4 per user which I would also consider to be a success

Reflection

On Reflection of this project, I believe that we created a much better site that massively increased the number of users submitting forms and using the site in general. I would have liked to run more A/B tests on the site as I believe we could have increased the conversion rate even higher if we had more data to work with

There is no next step unfortunately, due to the site having being shut down by the owners and the company dissolving due to unrelated circumstances
